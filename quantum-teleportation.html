A Basic Teleportation Circuit
Quantum teleportation is an essential circuit in quantum computing, which allows for long range entanglement and sharing of resources.
Quantum teleportation generates a resource, entangled state called a Bell State
The goal is to use distributed Maximally Entangled Resource States (Bell States) and classical communication to transmit a qubit from one party (Alice) to a second Party (Bob).

In a teleportation circuit, the measurement information needs to be transmitted to Bob over a classical channel. However, the main feature is that as long as Alice and Bob can generate pure maximally entangled states and distribute the components of the Bell state, they can transmit an unknown state without having to physically move it. This highlights the usefulness of maximally entangled states as a resource state for some sort of more useful computation or protocol.
